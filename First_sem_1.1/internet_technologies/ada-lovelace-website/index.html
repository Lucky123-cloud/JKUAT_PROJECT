Biography

Last Known Address: 12 St. James's Square, London, England

Education

Ada Lovelace was privately tutored in mathematics and science, which was unusual for women at the time.

Employment History

She worked closely with Charles Babbage on the Analytical Engine, a proposed mechanical general-purpose computer.

Other Relevant Information

Ada is often considered the first computer programmer due to her work on the Analytical Engine.

Awards and Distinctions

  • Recognized as the first computer programmer.
  • Ada Lovelace Day is celebrated annually to honor women in STEM.
